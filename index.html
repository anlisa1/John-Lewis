History

  • As a student, he partcipated in many sit-ins and at lunch counters and many other segregated public places, after he started his study on nonviolent protest. He went to jail for a sit in with his students at a lunch counter, after being harrased, spit on and beaten.

  • 1961 ---- particpated in freedom rides that challenged the segregation of interstate buses.
    _______ On a Montgomery bus terminal, he was attacked by a mob and beaten once again.

  • 1961 ----- Lewis was one of the founders of the Student Nonviolent Coordinating Committee (SNCC)

  • 1964 ----- Lewis coordinated SNCC’s efforts for the Mississippi Freedom Summer campaign, in order to register black voters across the South

  • 1965 ----- On march 7 known as the Bloody Sunday, where a march led by Lewis and Hosea Williams for voting right led to the protesters being beaten, as John Lewis skull was fratured, he got on TV.
